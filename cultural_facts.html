Spanish Holidays/Events
Event/Holiday Where When and Description
Año Nuevo Every Spanish speaking countries January 1st/Celebration of the beginning of the year. Fireworks, etc.
Carnival Most Spanish speaking countries Usually ends on the day before Ash Wednesday/A celebration before fasting associated with Lent. Parades, costumes, dances, Carnival Queen elections, and fireworks
Les Fogueres de Sant Joan Spain only June 19th to 24th/Fireworks, parties, big bonfires
Innaculada Concepción Spain Only December 8th/ Feast celebrating the birth of Mary, the mother of Jesus Christ.
La siesta Every Spanish speaking country Mid-afternoon nap/ Not as popular from modern changes, but still exists.
Cinco de Mayo Mexico May 5th; a holiday that celebrates the date of the Mexican army’s 1862 victory over France at the Battle of Puebla during the Franco-Mexican War.
Día de Muertos Mexico Octobber 31-November 2; The multi-day holiday focuses on gatherings of family and friends to pray for and remember friends and family members who have died, and help support their spiritual journey.
Spanish Cuisine
Food Where When/Description Picture
Paella Spain During the springtime festival, Las Fallas. It has rice meat or fish, saffron, paprika, and a variety of other spices.
Mantecados Spain, specifically in the southern regions Christmas tradition/Almond cake
Turrón Spain, specifically in the southern regions Christmas tradition/Candy made from almonds and honey
Plátanos Central and South America They are relatives of the banana but are starchier. Usually they are fried and served as a side dish.
Horchata Central America, Mexico, Spain A drink made with either almonds or rice. It is usually served warm (not chilled, but at room temperature).
